McKenzie Theory

New Zealand Physical Therapist Robin McKenzie (1931-2013) is renowned for developing the Mechanical Diagnosis and Therapy system, also known as the McKenzie Method.

McKenzie states that the patient must learn to manage future back pain by using a self-treatment and self-manipulation method. He also recommends that treatment be implemented during an attack of low back pain rather than after it has subsided in order to educate or teach preventative methods. These types of patients do not suffer from serious pathology. However, the lower back pain often becomes more severe with each successive attack.

In McKenzie Theory, when pain is more central—closer to the spine—it is less serious than peripheral pain—farther away from the spine. To treat back pain, then, we want to try to do exercises or movements that cause the pain to move closer to the spine. One can determined immediately when the Pyraback™ is providing a benefit because it should centralize the pain, moving it closer to the spine.

Trigger Point Theory

A trigger point feels like a knot or a small lump that can range in size from a pinhead to a pea. In the long muscles, a trigger point feels like a short piece of cord and can easily be mistaken for a tendon.

During massage, the trigger point will be painful, but the pain is necessary to return the muscle to its normal state.

The Pyraback™ with its pyramid points in contact with the paraspinal muscles will function exactly as a human hand to release those trigger points.

  • How Does the Pyraback™ Work?

    Full back flexion will decrease the pressure within the facets of the apophysial joints. This will reduce the pressure load in the intervertebral disc, but because long periods of flexion paradoxically can affect the disc, It is advised to do special exercises combined with extension. During the extension, the disc will displace forward. Considering the line of forces caused by the body weight and the grade of thoracic kyphosis, is important to start locating the Pyraback™ first at the kidney level (just across the place where the lower ribs end—thoracic vertebrae T10 to lumbar vertebra L1). This will reduce not only the thoracic flexion torque, but also the cervical flexion torque. This will eliminate the internal moment arm for trunk extensor muscles forces. At this point, the Pyraback™ will be treating trigger points by squeezing the lactic acid from the muscle fiber. As we all know, stretching will contribute to improve circulation and eliminate tension.

  • How Do I Use Pyraback™?

    Use Pyraback™ while laying with your knees bent or stretched, while sitting when working, driving, or watching TV.

  • How Often Should I Use Pyraback™?

    You should use this technology as you need it, but not more than 15 minutes in each session if applied when laying down. The Pyraback™ can be used once or twice in a day if the symptoms of back pain are mild. However, once you start using it you will realize and feel how much treatment you need and what part of the body will benefit the most. Remember to check your skin regularly.

  • Contraindications

    Please do not use the Pyraback™ device under these circumstances:

    • If you do not know the cause of your back pain.
    • The device should not be used for an individual where back extension is contraindicated, as in the McKenzie Therapy. This is due to the fact that the device promotes back extension.
    • The device should not be used by people for whom no position or movement can be found to reduce or centralize pain patterns. If the individual’s back pain is not reduced with any position or movement, do not try to use this device at this time.
    • Do not use the device f there is a suspicion of serious pathology where there has been no apparent reason for the onset of symptoms.
    • Do not use the device if this pain has been present for many weeks or months, and during that time has increased in intensity.
    • The device should not be used by those individuals who exhibit signs of extreme pain where the spine freezes or immobilizes when palpation is attempted, at least during this stage.
    • The device should not be used directly over scar tissue not well healed, or over damaged or irritated skin. (It is recommended you use a layer of cloth when using this device to prevent skin irritation).
  • Maintenance

    The Pyraback™ can be washed with soap and water. It is made with a special rubber that resists extremes temperatures (cold or hot). However, any sharp object (scissors, knife, etc) could tear it.

  • Cautionary Advice

    The following advice should be noted:

    • You should not use the Pyraback™ lying down if you are pregnant. You still can use it in a sitting position but not beyond the sixth month of pregnancy.
    • You should use the Pyraback™ in sitting position if the trigger points in your back are super-sensitive or extremely active. You can try to use it as tolerated.
